# Homeroom AI Guardrails

## Observations, Not Labels

Homeroom never saves fixed trait labels as learner memory. Raw notes may contain messy teacher language, but saved observations describe behavior in context and time.

Example:
- Raw note: `Maya is quiet in math class.`
- Saved observation: `In math class, Maya spoke less during the moment the teacher noticed; more context is needed before carrying this forward.`

## Evidence Required

Every generated observation, insight, pattern, pathway, and handoff claim must cite source evidence. If evidence is thin, confidence is low or the item is marked for revalidation.

No evidence means no saved insight.

## Hostile Or Vague Input

If a teacher enters an insult or broad judgment, Homeroom must not repeat it as a claim about the student. It asks for the observable moment instead.

Example:
- Teacher: `student sucks`
- Homeroom: `What happened today that made you feel concerned?`

If no specific evidence is provided, the observation preview must say there is not enough evidence to save.

## Conflict As Signal

Contradictions are not averaged into a trait. If one setting shows low full-class visibility and another shows strong prepared or small-group work, the pattern is context dependence.

Demo pattern:
`Participation is context-dependent.`

Demo support:
`Try prepared discussion roles, write-before-speak prompts, recurring opener slots, and avoid cold-calling without warm-up.`

## Parent And Student Visibility

Parents see strengths, `Nurture Talents`, conversation prompts, and home supports. They never see raw notes, teacher-only observations, Growth Map, internal patterns, or predictive pathway copy.

Students see a safe self-profile: interests, books, activities, and Big Five/IPIP-style learner reflection. They never see teacher observations, Growth Map, pathways, internal notes, or evidence trails.

## Handoff Gating

A new teacher starts with a fresh-start briefing: grades, strengths, learning style, and things to observe. Deeper patterns unlock only after the teacher logs their own first observation, with a demo reveal available for judging time.

## Personality Reflection

Big Five/IPIP-style output is student-owned self-reflection, not diagnosis and not a permanent label. Copy must say it can be retaken or edited.

## AI Is Not Allowed To

- Diagnose a student.
- Predict a career or say a student should become something.
- Save unsupported student-specific facts.
- Convert a label or insult into a permanent learner trait.
- Expose raw teacher notes to parents or students.
- Treat low participation visibility as low understanding.
- Save AI output without human approval.
